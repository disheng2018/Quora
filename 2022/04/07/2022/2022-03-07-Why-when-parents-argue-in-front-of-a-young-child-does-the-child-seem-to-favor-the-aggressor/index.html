Why, when parents argue in front of a young child, does the child seem to favor the aggressor

Diane Cavaness, I taught middle school science for 37 years

1M views · 9.8KUpvotes

1964, I’m 8, my brothers are 6 and 10. After some hours of the the usual screaming and abuse, he comes and gets us all out of bed, lines us up on the couch, and says, “Who do you love best, your mother or me?” I can still see my mother hovering in the background.

One by one, we betray her, telling him we love him best. And the lie worked. It got us back to the relative safety of our beds.

We “sided“ with him because we were terrified of him.
